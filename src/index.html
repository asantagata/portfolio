    Alex Santagata (/sæntəˈɡɒtə/, san-tə-GAHT-ə; born February 25, 2004) is an American web developer. He is a senior at Worcester Polytechnic Institute[1], where he studies software engineering, artificial intelligence, and high-performance computing (HPC).

    Primarily a front-end developer, Alex's work focuses on combining beautiful user interfaces with significant functionality. With over ten years of experience writing code for the web, he has developed mastery over React, JavaScript, and plain old HTML. Alex is proficient in:

    • The following languages: JavaScript, TypeScript, C, C++, Java, Python, MATLAB, Racket
    • The following frontend utilities: HTML, CSS, React, Svelte, Astro, GSAP, Tailwind, Bootstrap
    • The following database and backend utilities: SQL, Postgres, Node.js, Socket.IO, WebSockets, Axios, Prisma, Sequelize
    • And the following tools and platforms: Git, Linux, Visual Studio Code, WebStorm, IntelliJ, Trello

    Professional work

    As a member of Mosaiq software, a collective of WPI-based web developers[2], Alex is currently working on Mint, a Svelte-based image compositor which includes advanced features such as layer transformations, project persistence, and image collages.

    Alex's recent work includes Card Domain, a real-time multiplayer card gaming platform with a rich suite of features including fully customizable deck design and distribution. Alex developed the full-stack architecture of Card Domain over the summer of 2025, utilizing Socket.IO and Sequelize for real-time and persistent interactions.

    He also contributed to the development of Terrazzo, an online application for kanban-style list-making. Terrazzo includes an impressive breadth of features such as live WebSockets-based interactions and an extensive in-house markdown system which Alex developed.

    Other Mosaiq projects of note include the Mosaiq portfolio page, a stylish presentation of selected works by Mosaiq.

    Academic work

    Alex is currently working with a team at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory to develop PerfFlowAspect, an LLVM-based profiling tool for HPC programs in C++ and Python based on the aspect-oriented programming paradigm. Alex is working on various features for PFA, including porting the project to later versions of the Clang compiler and creating benchmark workflows.

    Some of Alex's notable academic work is his significant contribution to the Brigham & Women's Hospital web app, developed in a team of ten engineers over the course of spring 2025. In addition to managing the team with Agile methodologies, Alex designed crucial features such as the navigation system, the editor for the hospital floor plan, a forum, and voice command processing. Other courses of interest include Machine Learning, Analysis of Algorithms, and Theory of Computation.

    Alex's work also extends beyond the contiguous United States. Over the fall of 2024, Alex worked alongside a team of GIS developers and the Department of Public Works of Nantucket, Massachusetts, to develop, populate and analyze a database and GIS layer describing Nantucket's town-owned trees.

    Personal life

    Alex has also tutored in programming, mathematics and physics for several years. On top of his expertise in traditional web development technologies, he has a passion for formal language processing and related theory, and enjoys teaching and integrating these into his methodologies for web development.

    Outside of the field of computing, Alex is passionate about tabletop games, which inspired several of his software projects. He also enjoys instrumental indie rock, convoluted audio dramas, and puzzle games such as those in Simon Tatham's puzzle collection.
